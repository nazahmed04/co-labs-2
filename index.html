Charities making a change

Lifeshare

Life share is the oldest charity dedicated to the Homeless in Manchester. Their objectives include; identifying street homelessness and offering assistance in obtaining emergency accommodation, signposting other relevant agencies and information for support, alleviating aspects of poverty and hardship through redistribution of resources. By promoting health and harm reduction, they assist those in need of access Primary Health Care.

Barnabus

Barnabas is a Christian Homeless Charity in Manchester for over 30 years. Their drop-in centre offers food, showers, clothing, access to medical care and activities. Barnabas also host a support office in which they offer accommodation referrals, advice resettlement, and employment. In 2005 Barnabas received Queen Elizabeth II's "Unsung Heroes" award for Voluntary Service.

Coffee4Craig

Coffee4Craig is a homeless provision that provides hot, home-cooked meals, access to medical support, shower facilities, fundraisers and much more every evening. This organisation is dedicated to Craig White who tragically passed in 2013 and began as a reaction to his death. With the help of Northwest first aid in 2014, Coffee4Craig started the first street kitchen in the heart of Manchester, Piccadilly Gardens.

Centrepoint Manchester

Since 2017, Centrepoint has been supporting 16–25-year-olds in Manchester. Through housing and healthcare to education and engagement, Centrepoint aims to help young people from rough sleeping to a bright future. By operating with 60 services around the UK, Centrepoint aims to end youth homelessness by 2037 with 94% of their young people have moved on safely and positively.
